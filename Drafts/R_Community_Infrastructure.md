## Technical Report: Strategic Assessment of Community Network Sponsorship

**Date:** April 24, 2026
**Subject:** Transition Strategy for Community Network Platforms
**Project Status:** Active / Optimization Phase

---

### 1. Executive Summary
This report outlines the current operational framework for the community network and proposes a strategic migration from **meetup.com** to **luma.com**. Historically, the network has relied exclusively on Meetup for group management and event coordination. Given the **R Consortium’s** intent to **massively curtail expenditure**, a transition to Luma is recommended as a cost-effective alternative to sustain the global network.

---

### 2. Historical Infrastructure and Sponsorship
To date, the **Meetup Pro Network** has been the sole platform utilized for managing community engagement. This has been fully sponsored by the **R Consortium**, providing "Pro" level licensing for a centralized hierarchy of chapters.

While effective for discovery, the enterprise-level costs associated with Meetup Pro have become unsustainable under new budgetary directives.

---

### 3. Current Annual Expenditure (Meetup Pro)
The R Consortium currently subsidizes 347 chapters. Based on standard enterprise pricing for Meetup Pro (estimated at **$30 – $35 per group/month**), the annual financial commitment is substantial.

| Chapter Type | Count | Est. Monthly Cost (Total) | Est. Annual Cost (Total) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **R-Ladies Chapters** | 250 | $7,500 – $8,750 | $90,000 – $105,000 |
| **RUGS Chapters** | 97 | $2,910 – $3,395 | $34,920 – $40,740 |
| **Total Network** | **347** | **$10,410 – $12,145** | **$124,920 – $145,740** |

---

### 4. Financial Forecast and Proposed Pivot
Internal communications indicate that the **R Consortium** will, in all probability, drastically reduce funding for community sponsorships. To mitigate the impact of these cuts, it is proposed that the network migrates to **luma.com**.

**The Case for Luma:**
* **Reduced Overhead:** Luma offers a more flexible pricing structure that significantly undercuts the per-group cost of Meetup Pro.
* **Scalability:** Luma’s platform allows for efficient management of multiple chapters without the steep incremental costs associated with Meetup’s "Pro" seats.
* **Integration:** Enhanced tools for registration and analytics are included at lower price tiers, offering better value for the reduced budget.

---

### 5. Operational Requirements for Transition
In response to the mandate for fiscal austerity, the following steps are required:

1.  **Decommissioning Meetup Pro:** A phased sunsetting of Meetup subscriptions to eliminate high-cost recurring fees.
2.  **Migration to Luma:** Onboarding the 347 chapters to Luma to maintain community continuity at a fraction of the previous cost.
3.  **Expenditure Justification:** Finalizing the new budget to demonstrate the significant savings achieved through this platform shift.

---

### 6. Conclusion
Continuing with the historical reliance on Meetup is no longer viable. By pivoting to **luma.com**, the community can maintain its global footprint while adhering to the R Consortium's new expenditure constraints. This transition ensures that R-Ladies and RUGS chapters remain supported despite the reduction in available funding.
