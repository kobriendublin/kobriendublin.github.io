
Kevin O'Brien's Professional Background
Kevin O'Brien has extensive experience in technology conference management and community planning. He believes he can leverage his skills to contribute to a strong, startup-friendly tech ecosystem that benefits local enterprises and SMEs.

He was recognized as a Python Software Foundation Fellow in 2020 for his contributions to PyCon conferences in Dublin and Limerick. Other PyCons, including those in Cork and Galway, faced opposition.

Kevin was the conference lead for the tech conference segment of Career Zoo in February 2020 at Fáilte Park. He is an active member of the PyData Global Organizing Team, a virtual conference group. He is also involved in organizing I Did in London and I Did in New York, scheduled for January and June 2025 in London's Paternoster Square and Times Square, New York. The New York event will be held at Microsoft's Conference Center.

His roles in these events include managing social media accounts, securing small sponsors, corporate outreach, recruiting speakers, and supervising sessions. His work has given him extensive corporate and tech-world exposure, making him well-positioned to nurture a thriving ecosystem for innovation in life sciences, food sciences, and agricultural technology.

Conference Leadership and Organizing Efforts
Kevin served as the coordinator for the Why R? webinar series, focusing on the statistical programming language R, widely used in life sciences. He is also part of the Uzar Conference Organizing Team.

He has a background in theater festival events, particularly audience development, brand strategy, social media, and PR outreach. In later years, he worked extensively with the Jameson Dublin International Philosophical Society, maximizing conference outreach through social media. His expertise later transitioned into technology communities such as Python and Julia.

A key concern for open-source technology communities is their reliance on social media tools like Twitter and Meetup, both of which have significantly declined in effectiveness over the past three to four years. Open-source organizations are now struggling to fill the engagement gap. One of Kevin’s achievements in this area was successfully rebuilding the Rozer Community LinkedIn page, successfully migrating the RE User Community to LinkedIn as an aggregator.

JuliaCon Contributions
Kevin served as Social Media Chair for JuliaCon from 2017 to 2023, overseeing multiple conferences. One of the most successful was the 2021 Virtual JuliaCon, which saw nearly 29,000 registrations and 10,000 confirmed attendees. This event marked a turning point in Julia's acceptance as a mainstream programming tool.

Due to departmental restructuring, his position ended in early 2024. He is now highly motivated to pursue a new role in technology while reducing travel commitments.
