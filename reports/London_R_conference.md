# PROPOSAL: R-Day London 2026
**"Reclaiming the 'R' in Pirate Day"**

**Date:** Saturday, September 19, 2026  
**Venue:** London South Bank University (LSBU), Southwark Campus  
**Social Venue:** The Elephant & Castle (Basement Venue)  
**Theme:** Data Science, Open Source Governance, and Community Literacy in R

---

## 1. Executive Summary
The proposed **R-Day London 2026** is a one-day technical conference aimed at the UK’s data science and statistical computing community. By strategically launching on **September 19** (International Talk Like a Pirate Day), the event seeks to "reclaim" the day as a celebration of the **R programming language**. The conference will facilitate knowledge exchange between academia and industry, featuring a curated lineup of speakers at LSBU, followed by a community networking event at the nearby Elephant & Castle.

## 2. Objectives
*   **Community Consolidation:** Bring together London’s diverse R users—from finance and pharma to academia and public policy.
*   **Reclamation Branding:** Establish a memorable, annual fixture in the tech calendar by pivoting a popular culture event into a technical milestone.
*   **Professional Development:** Deliver high-quality technical talks on the R ecosystem, including Shiny, Tidyverse, and Bioconductor.

## 3. Venue & Logistics

### 3.1 Primary Technical Venue: LSBU
The Southwark Campus offers state-of-the-art facilities suitable for a professional technical gathering:
*   **Main Track:** Southwark Lecture Theatre (Capacity: ~260).
*   **Networking/Catering:** The Exhibition Space (Capacity: ~300).
*   **Accessibility:** Centrally located with high-speed Wi-Fi and full AV support for recording sessions.

### 3.2 Post-Conference Social: The Elephant & Castle
To foster informal networking, the event will transition to the **Elephant & Castle** pub, located a 5-minute walk from the campus. 
*   **Private Space:** The dedicated **Basement Bar** has been identified for its capacity (approx. 100-120) and exclusive atmosphere.
*   **Functional Use:** This space will host the "Pirate Social," allowing sponsors and attendees to connect in a relaxed setting.

## 4. Program Structure (Proposed)
*   **09:00 - 09:30:** Registration and Coffee (Exhibition Space)
*   **09:30 - 12:30:** Morning Technical Sessions (Lecture Theatre)
*   **12:30 - 13:30:** Networking Lunch & Sponsor Booths
*   **13:30 - 16:30:** Afternoon Workshops and Lightning Talks
*   **17:00 - Late:** The "Arr-Afterparty" (Elephant & Castle Basement)

## 6. Strategic Rationale
September 19, 2026, falls on a Saturday, providing the ideal opportunity for a full-day community event without conflicting with the standard work week. Historically, major tech publishers (like O'Reilly) and the R Consortium have recognized the "R" connection to Pirate Day; this event formalizes that connection into a physical, high-impact conference in one of the world's leading data hubs.
